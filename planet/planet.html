ABOUT THIS GUIDE
Biroc's Guide to the Planets, a recognized source for information about all the inhabited worlds, has been published for over two-hundred years. It began as Biroc's Shipping and Transporation Time-table Guide to Quardrant I in 2592, and within a few years had been expanded to cover the entire Empire.
Notes on using this guide.
The information in this guide is organized hier-archically. Quadrants are the main spatial organ-ization of the Empire. Within each quadrant will be found a list of states, and within each state a list of inhabited worlds that fall under that state's juris-diction. Planets that are not assigned to any state are listed separately within each quadrant. Stat-istical information about quadrants and states are provided, as are statistics about individual planets. Notable structures and institutions are also listed, as are any navigational hazards present in the area. Please see DEFINITIONS for more information about the statistics reported.

The editors of this giude strive to provide as ac-curate and up-to-date information as possible, with new editions being issued quarterly. If you find any mistake or omissions, please contact our offices.

QUADRANTS
  The Empire is divided into eight Quadrants*, numbered I - VIII, forming a sphere centered on the system of MARA, also known of as "The Center." Consider a sphere bisected by three orthogonal planes to imagine how the Empire is divided. The Great Rift defines the edge of this sphere around Quadrants VII and VIII.

  Political regions are assigned to specific quadrants, based upon the location of their traditional capital, and while some border states may have planets that technically cross into a different quadrant, those planets are typically grouped within their political re-gion's quadrant.

  Select a link to view the regions and unassigned planets located within that quad-rant.
* The editors of this guide are aware of the linguistically inaccurate use of the term "quadrant" to describe a sphere divided into eighths. While "octant" may not have been acceptable to readers, certainly "sector" would have been a better choice. However, the use of the term "quadrant" to describe the eight sectors has been firmly established since ancient times, and we see no reason to attempt to change the usage, despite its obvious incorrectness.

  The origins of this lamentable abuse of language are obscure, but tradition holds that Emp. Juron II (d. 241), feeling that the division of the Empire into four quadrants was insufficient, doubled their number. However, recent research indicates that the division of the universe into eigth sectors was practiced within the pre-Imperial Maran kingdoms.
